About Me

Decoding the Genome

I am Nathish Lakshman, a PhD Scholar at the Department of Human Genetics and Molecular Biology, Bharathiar University, Coimbatore. My doctoral research investigates the genetic and transcriptomic underpinnings of neurological and neuropsychiatric conditions.

My work spans next-generation sequencing (NGS) data analysis, differential gene expression profiling, and cellular models of neurodegeneration. I work with PARK7 gene mutation in SH-SY5Y neuroblastoma cells, explore pseudogene regulatory roles in cancer, and investigate the oro-pharyngeal microbiome–immune axis in schizophrenia.

I believe science advances through collaboration. My goal is to translate genomic discoveries into mechanistic insights that can eventually inform therapeutic strategies for complex neurological diseases.

Research Interests

🧬
Neurogenetics & Gene Mutation
PARK7 mutation analysis in Parkinson's disease; gene variant discovery in SH-SY5Y human neuroblastoma cell models and iPSC-derived neurons.
⚡
Oxidative Stress Pathways
ROS-mediated neurodegeneration; neuroprotective potential of Convolvulus pluricaulis; H₂O₂-induced cellular damage models and antioxidant signalling.
📈
Transcriptomics & RNA-Seq
NGS data pipelines (DESeq2, edgeR, GATK); pseudogene regulatory roles (CDC27P9); differential gene expression in cervical cancer and neurological disease.
🦠
Neuropsychiatric Genomics
Oro-pharyngeal microbiome–immune crosstalk in schizophrenia; microbiota-gut-brain axis; neuro-inflammatory gene networks and cytokine profiling.
